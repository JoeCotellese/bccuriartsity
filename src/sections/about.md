---
I think interesting people build interesting spaces. And interesting spaces
need pieces with a story — things that make you curious, that start
conversations, that surprise you because you're just now waking up to the
idea that beauty comes in many forms.

We all contain multitudes. Your space should, too.

Mass market art and decor can be lovely. I've certainly bought plenty of it
and liked it well enough. But there's nothing like going on a treasure hunt
and coming home with something you're genuinely excited about. And in today's
world of AI everything, I'm even more drawn to things that were created for
humans, by humans.

I hunt for the pieces that feel rare and thoughtful — objects with a real
maker's hand behind them. The things that catch my eye usually have something
unexpected about them, a weird angle, a color that shouldn't work but does.
I'm drawn to work that has texture, personality, and won't apologize for being
strange.
